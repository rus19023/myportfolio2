What is granularity?

Let's compare building a data warehouse to a bakery. There can be many different items in the bakery: cakes, breads, cookies, etc. If you don't include all the ingredients, in the correct measurements, and follow each recipe's instructions using the correct utensils, the baked goods will fail to rise, or taste bad, or look ugly. Every day the baker makes many baked goods. He or she would need to calculate the quantity of each ingredient at scale needed for each of the completed goods, baked into differently shaped pans. The basic recipes would define the granularity, the least amount of each ingredient, each having the correct measurement, specific, ordered instructions to create a perfect product, no matter what shape or size it might end up.

"Recipes" are also needed to design each OLAP cube or data mart, which are like the individual finished baked products. If you don't include all the ingredients (fact tables) in the right measurements (dimensions) and follow the instructions (use appropriate ETL Tools), the cubes will also fail to rise (not have all the layers, rows and columns), taste bad (not have the needed totals and subtotals) or just look ugly (have holes in the data slices and/or dices).

Bakers will have experimented with different variables: measurements, similar ingredients, bake times, etc, to come up with their perfect version. They have spent many hours, used many ingredients, and thrown away many finished products before that perfect version. Similarly, a data mart recipe would need to be developed before the database is baked (filled with data for reports and analysis).

The myriad ETL tools available today help to not "reinvent the wheel" when it comes to designing a data warehouse. However, just as the baker, before the baking begins, the database developer will need to fully understand the lowest proportions of ingredients (smallest grain) to fit the needs of the business purposes for the cube, then design the correct recipe using said granularity for their data store "recipe", to avoid wasted time and money.
